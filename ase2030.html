Course Info.

Course descriptions

  • This course is about two central mathematical techniques for all engineering disciplines: linear algebra and statistics. The course covers basics of vectors and matrices, solving linear equations, least-squares methods, probability, statistics, and many applications. We talk about the mathematics, but the focus will be on conceptual understanding and using those in applications such as dynamics, control, estimation, and machine learning. Students are strongly encouraged to attend the optional computation sessions where they use Python to do computations with vectors, matrices, and random variables for solving the engineering problems.

Prerequisites

  • Basically no prerequisite required. Previous exposure to programming languages (Python or others) will be helpful for understanding the conceptual ideas.

  • You do not need to have any background knowledges on a variety of engineering application, however interest in them will definitely be a plus.

Reference textbooks

  • Introduction to applied linear algebra - vectors, matrices, and least squares, by Boyd and Vandenberghe. Available online.

  • Linear algebra and learning from data, by Strang.

  • Probability, random variables and random signal principles, by Peebles and Shi.
